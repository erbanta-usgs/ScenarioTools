Hydraulic conductance

 Top  Previous  Next

A measure of the ease with which groundwater flows through a finite volume of porous medium of given cross-sectional area and length. Hydraulic conductance is the product of hydraulic conductivity and cross-sectional area of flow divided by the length of the flow path. In MODFLOW, hydraulic conductance is defined for connections between adjacent model cells or between a model cell and an external, head-dependent source or sink of water. Units: L2T-1.
